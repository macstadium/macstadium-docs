MacStadium offers Mac mini in several Apple silicon configurations. Use this table to compare specs and find the right fit for your workload.

## Mac mini configurations

| Plan | Chip | CPU cores | GPU cores | RAM | SSD | Best for |
|------|------|-----------|-----------|-----|-----|----------|

All Mac mini models are Apple silicon (ARM). Intel Mac mini hardware is available for custom orders — [contact MacStadium sales](https://www.macstadium.com/contact/sales) for more.

Standard configurations are available for immediate purchase on the [MacStadium customer portal](https://portal.macstadium.com/register).

## Choosing a configuration

**For Orka clusters:** Any M4 or M2 Pro model works well as an Orka node. More RAM means more VMs per node — M4.L and M4.XL are recommended for teams running multiple concurrent VMs.

**For CI/CD pipelines:** Xcode build time scales closely with CPU performance. M4 Pro models complete large Xcode builds roughly 2x faster than the M2 baseline. See the [benchmarks page](/iaas/bare-metal-macs/macstadium-bare-metal-mac-benchmarks) for measured comparisons.

**For GPU workloads or AI/ML:** If GPU throughput is your priority, consider [Mac Studio](/iaas/bare-metal-macs/bare-metal-hosts), which is available with M2 Ultra for significantly higher GPU core counts.
